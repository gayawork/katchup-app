The Why

K-Culture deserves better than decontextualized noise.

There's a lot of K-Culture content out there. Most of it is unverified, decontextualized, or just plain confusing if you didn't grow up in Korea. Katchup exists to fix that.

Every week, a Korean insider reads through what actually matters, not just what went viral. Stories get verified against Korean-language sources. Context that non-Korean readers would miss gets added in. The result is a tight digest you can finish in three minutes and actually trust.

The newsletter started because K-Culture context is half the story, and most coverage skips it. Katchup is two things: the website is a curated link archive, always current. The newsletter is where the real value lives, social context, cultural nuance, and the occasional Korean expression that shows you what's really going on behind the headline.
